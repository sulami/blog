>> Linux-powered alarm clock without a RasPi

Di 11 Juni 2013 | in Linux | tags: cron mplayer onlineradio rtcwake

My method of using the Raspberry Pi as alarm clock with mplayer-cronjobs is also usable on regular desktop machines. To overcome the wasted power and noise while sleeping, the computer is going to sleep as well, suspending to either ram, disk or both. Then you can use rtcwake to let the hardware clock wake it up automatically just a minute before alarm time.

sudo rtcwake -m no -l -t $(date +%s =d'tomorrow 07:30')

What this does is it prepares the hardware clock to wake, but does not initiate the sleep yet, so you can continue using the computer and bring it down later, before going to sleep. It is also told that the hardware clock is using local time (-l) and not UTC (-u) and is given a unix-timestamp by date asÂ the alarm time. For optimal results, have your PC wake up a minute before you, so the cronjob does not get cut off.
