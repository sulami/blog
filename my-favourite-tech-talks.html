My favourite Tech-Talks

Published: Sat 25 January 2014

In misc.

I love talks, others might call them presentations. There are thousands of talks on YouTube and watching them all would take ages, but I sometimes watch talks before going to bed instad of watching TV.  So I thought, I could compile a small list of my favourite talks featuring technical topics for myself and you readers.

  • And that's how I lost my eye: exploring emergeny data destruction (2012) - case studies on how to unrecoverably destroy your data within seconds

  • Git for ages 4 and up (2013) - explains git basics really easy

  • I wanna be the guy: The arduous path to senior sysadmin (2013) - best practices and advice on how to be a better sysadmin

  • No-Tech Hacking (2008) - real life hacking without use of advanced technology, quite funny, too

  • Pwned by the owner (2010) - funny story of a hacker whose PC gets stolen and how he got it back

  • SELinux for mere mortals (2013) - what SELinux does, why to use it and how to use it

  • The real story behind Wayland and X (2013) - a Wayland-dev explains why X sucks from a technical view and what Wayland actually does

  • Why Linux sucks / Why Linux does not suck (2012) - watch both in this order, funny comment on the state of Linux

  • Write and submit your first Linux kernel patch (2012) - how to get started on kernel development

There are a lot more good talks, but my time is sort of limited and sadly I can't really focus on working while watching talks.
