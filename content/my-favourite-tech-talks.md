Title: My favourite Tech-Talks
Date: 2014-01-25 14:49
Category: Web
Tags: git, talks, youtube, wayland, kernel, selinux

I love talks, others might call them presentations. There are thousands
of talks on YouTube and watching them all would take ages, but I
sometimes watch talks before going to bed instad of watching TV.  So I
thought, I could compile a small list of my favourite talks featuring
technical topics for myself and you readers.

* [And that's how I lost my eye: exploring emergeny data destruction
(2012)][1] - case studies on how to unrecoverably destroy your data
within seconds

* [Git for ages 4 and up (2013)][2] - explains git basics really easy

* [I wanna be the guy: The arduous path to senior sysadmin (2013)][3] -
best practices and advice on how to be a better sysadmin

* [No-Tech Hacking (2008)][4] - real life hacking without use of advanced
technology, quite funny, too

* [Pwned by the owner (2010)][5] - funny story of a hacker whose PC gets
stolen and how he got it back

* [SELinux for mere mortals (2013)][6] - what SELinux does, why to use it
and how to use it

* [The real story behind Wayland and X (2013)][7] - a Wayland-dev explains
why X sucks from a technical view and what Wayland actually does

* [Why Linux sucks][8] / [Why Linux does not suck (2012)][9] - watch both in
this order, funny comment on the state of Linux

* [Write and submit your first Linux kernel patch (2012)][10] - how to get
started on kernel development

There are a lot more good talks, but my time is sort of limited and
sadly I can't really focus on working while watching talks.
